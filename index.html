    Top 3 Test Automation Challenges I have faced

    1. Stakeholder Buyin üóÑÔ∏è

    Before framework adoption, we had some convincing to do for the engineering team to understand the pros of building an automation suite an integrating it to the CI/CD pipeline. By outlining the advantages of testing early and often and faster feedback and time to market we were able to champion for adoption and usage. This was accomplished by doing research on the best tooling options and by delivering a presentation of the preferred option in an engineering architectural session.

    2. Flaky Tests üèóÔ∏è

    A major challenge i have encountered was test flakiness in Cypress due to asynchronous DOM operations, which hindered timely feature releases and impacted product quality. We addressed this by adopting best practices and replacing fragile selectors with robust data-cy attributes. This experience honed my adaptability and improved my testing approach. We proactively mitigated test flakiness by using Cypress's retry mechanism. This mechanism automatically retries failed tests, enhancing reliability and boosting confidence in our testing process. To optimize wait times, we shifted from implicit to explicit waits. Explicit waits proved to be more precise and efficient, pausing execution until specific conditions are met.

    3. Cross-platform Testing üîÑ

    Last but not least, my experience with platform testing has brought to the fore a challenge of verification across devices and browsers and the risk of alienating large customer bases and eventually the baseline in terms of revenue. Ensuring consistent behavior across these spheres proves an uphill task if done manually. Therefore adoption of automated tools for example Firebase and Browserstack has been helpful. I have seen the benefits of using data as well to narrow down to most used devices/browsers to speed up test execution and reporting.

    Simplicity

    A no-code tool is a great option to allow users to manage use cases for example adopting a record and playback tool enables non-technical stakeholders to execute the user flows easily for example by using Katalon Studio. I have witnessed product managers being empowered to smoothly coordinate user acceptance testing sessions.

    üîß

    Behaviour Driven Development Philosophy

    Use of given, when, and then format when writing user acceptance criteria can allow less experienced people to test using a natural language making it easier and faster for Product, dev and QA teams to collaborate on features. I have seen quality metrics improve by using this development/testing methodology.

    Knowledge Management

    Adopting a tool that is well documented and has clear guidelines on setup, usage and troubleshooting is an excellent way for non-technical users to onboard and fastrack the testing process. My experience with stable open source tools that have an active support community has been very beneficial in championing for usage among engineering stakeholders. This has increased uptake and helped demystify the technical aspects of developement and testing.
